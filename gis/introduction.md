# What is GIS and its applications in real world
## 1. History of GIS
It was in the year 1854 when cholera hit the city of London, England Dr. John Snow drew points on the map for people effected with cholera and by interpretation, it striked to his mind that the possibility of outbreak could be from the contaminated water pump. The water pump is shut down and the outbreak ceased. The work of John Snow demonstrated that GIS is a problem-solving tool. He put geographic layers on a paper map and made a life-saving discovery...

![john snow](https://blog.rtwilson.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/SnowMap_Points.png)

Later in the early 1960’s Roger Tomlinson first introduced modern computerized geographic information systems. Maps have become important in our daily life. The use of location services has revolutionized.
## 2. What is GIS?
A geographic information system (GIS) is a computer-based tool to which can store, analyse and visualize the data.
## 3. Components of GIS
There are three main components of GIS:
#### Data

The GIS data is of two types: raster data and vector data.

- **Raster Data**

    The raster data contains in grids in the form of rows and columns. These each rows and columns are called as cells or pixels.

- **Vector Data**

    The vector data consists of points, lines or polygons. These data is obtained by interpreting the raster data and this data can save the additional data in the attribute table
    
#### Hardware
The hardware component is where the GIS software is used on. The configuration of CPU plays an important role in the speed for processing the data.
#### Software
The data can be created, updated, deleted and analysed using the GIS softwares. ArcGIS and QGIS are the leading softwares in the GIS. ArcGIS is the commercial software whereas QGIS is open sourced. The spatial analysis can be done by this softwares
